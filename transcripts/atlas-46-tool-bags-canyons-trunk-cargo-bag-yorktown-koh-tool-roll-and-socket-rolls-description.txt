WHY KOH?!
The King of the Hammers was founded by Jeff Knoll and Dave Cole in 2007, planned in a bar on a napkin. The founders decided that there should be a trial run with a few good guys and a chance to have some fun. There were only 12 teams at this first non-spectator invitational race, referred to as the OG13. The OG13 are always invited back to race year after year. All past winners, or Kings, are also invited year after year. The first official race was in 2008 and it was still a non-spectator race. There were approximately 50 drivers in the first official race. Race teams started at 30 second intervals from Means Dry Lake Bed at Johnson Valley, California and navigated through the desert on a 50-60 mile set race course to their first rock course. King of The Hammers race week has expanded from one race, King of The Hammers, held each year on Friday, to a series of 5 races held throughout the week. The races include King of The Motos on Sunday, The KOH UTV Race on Wednesday, The Every Man Challenge Race on Thursday, and a new ULTRA4 vs. Rock Bouncer shootout held on Monday night of race week.

What is a Mifflin?!
Thomas Mifflin (January 10, 1744 – January 20, 1800) was an American merchant and politician from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He served in a variety of roles during and after the American Revolution, several of which qualify him to be counted among the Founding Fathers. He was the first Governor of Pennsylvania, serving from 1790 to 1799.
